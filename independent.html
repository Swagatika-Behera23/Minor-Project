The Indian independence movement, a period of resistance against British colonial rule, spanned from the late 19th century to 1947, culminating in India's independence and the partition of the subcontinent.

Key Events and Movements:

Early Resistance:

The seeds of the Indian independence movement were sown with the Sepoy Mutiny of 1857, though it was not successful, it ignited the desire for self-rule.

Rise of Nationalism:

The Indian National Congress, founded in 1885, played a crucial role in organizing the movement and advocating for self-governance.

Gandhi's Leadership:

Mahatma Gandhi's non-violent resistance and movements like the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920), the Civil Disobedience Movement (including the Dandi March in 1930), and the Quit India Movement (1942) gained momentum for independence.

World War II's Impact:

The war weakened Britain's grip on India, and the Royal Indian Navy mutiny in 1946 further strained British control.

Partition and Independence:

The Indian Independence Act of 1947, passed by the British Parliament, formally granted independence to India and Pakistan on August 15, 1947.

Partition's Aftermath:

The partition led to immense displacement and violence, with millions of people migrating across the newly formed borders.

Formation of the Dominion of India:

Jawaharlal Nehru became the first Prime Minister of the Dominion of India, which was a member of the British Commonwealth.

Establishment of the Republic:

India became a democratic republic on January 26, 1950, with a Westminster-style parliamentary system.
